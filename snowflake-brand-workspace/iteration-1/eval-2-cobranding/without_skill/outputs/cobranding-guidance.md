# Co-Branding Guidance: Your Company + Snowflake

This document provides general guidance for creating a co-branded slide deck featuring your company logo alongside Snowflake's, based on publicly known Snowflake brand practices. **Note:** This guidance is based on general knowledge of Snowflake's brand, not official brand guidelines. Always confirm with Snowflake's partner marketing team before publishing co-branded materials.

---

## 1. Snowflake Logo Usage

### Approved Logo
- Use the official Snowflake logo as provided by Snowflake's partner or brand team. Do not recreate, redraw, or alter the logo in any way.
- Snowflake's primary logo consists of the snowflake icon (a stylized geometric snowflake shape) and the wordmark "snowflake" in lowercase.
- The logo is typically available in two orientations: horizontal (icon + wordmark side by side) and stacked (icon above wordmark).

### Logo Colors
- **Primary blue:** Snowflake's signature blue (approximately #29B5E8) is a core brand element.
- The logo should be used in its original brand-blue color on light backgrounds, or in white on dark backgrounds.
- Do not change the logo color to match your own brand palette.
- A monochrome (black or white) version may be acceptable when color printing is not available, but confirm with Snowflake.

### Clear Space and Minimum Size
- Maintain adequate clear space around the Snowflake logo. A general rule of thumb is to keep a margin equal to at least the height of the "s" in the snowflake wordmark on all sides.
- Do not place other logos, text, or graphic elements within the clear space zone.
- Do not reduce the logo below a minimum legible size (typically around 1 inch / 25 mm wide for print, or roughly 100 px wide for digital).

### Prohibited Treatments
- Do not stretch, distort, rotate, or skew the logo.
- Do not add effects such as drop shadows, gradients, glows, or outlines.
- Do not place the logo on busy or low-contrast backgrounds that reduce legibility.
- Do not crop or partially obscure any part of the logo.
- Do not animate the logo without explicit permission.
- Do not combine the Snowflake logo with your logo into a single merged mark or lockup unless explicitly approved by Snowflake.

## 2. Co-Branding Layout Rules

### Logo Placement on Slides
- **Title slide:** Place both logos on the title slide. A common approach is to position your company logo on the left and the Snowflake logo on the right (or vice versa), separated by adequate space or a thin vertical divider line.
- **Interior slides:** Depending on the context, you may place a smaller version of both logos in the footer or header area of each slide.
- **Closing slide:** Repeat both logos, similar to the title slide layout.

### Visual Hierarchy and Parity
- In a co-branded deck, both logos should be given equal visual weight. Neither logo should appear significantly larger or more prominent than the other.
- Align logos along a common baseline or center axis for visual consistency.
- If using a divider between logos, keep it subtle (a thin gray or neutral-colored line).

### Logo Separation
- Do not merge, overlap, or interlock the two logos.
- Keep a clear visual separation between them. A general minimum distance is roughly 2x the clear space requirement of the larger logo.

---

## 3. Color Palette and Typography

### Color Usage
- You may use Snowflake's brand blue (#29B5E8) as an accent color in charts, highlights, or design elements that reference Snowflake specifically.
- Your own brand colors should dominate the overall deck design if you are the primary author. If the deck is jointly authored, find a harmonious balance between both palettes.
- Avoid using colors that clash with or distort Snowflake's blue. Pair it with neutral tones (white, light gray, dark gray/charcoal) for best results.

### Typography
- Snowflake has historically used clean, modern sans-serif typefaces.
- For co-branded materials, use a neutral sans-serif font that complements both brands (e.g., your own brand font, or a universally available option like Arial, Calibri, or Helvetica).
- Do not attempt to replicate Snowflake's proprietary brand typeface unless they provide it to you for this purpose.

---

## 4. Messaging and Content Guidelines

### Brand Voice
- Snowflake's brand voice is generally confident, clear, and technically informed but accessible. Avoid overly casual or hyperbolic language when representing Snowflake's capabilities.
- Do not make claims about Snowflake's product capabilities, roadmap, or pricing without Snowflake's explicit approval.

### Trademark and Legal
- "Snowflake" is a registered trademark. Use the appropriate trademark symbol (typically the registered trademark symbol) on first prominent use in the deck, following Snowflake's trademark usage guidelines.
- Include a trademark attribution footnote on the closing slide or in the deck's fine print, such as: "Snowflake, the Snowflake logo, and related marks are trademarks or registered trademarks of Snowflake Inc. All other trademarks are the property of their respective owners."
- Do not use Snowflake's name or logo in a way that implies endorsement, sponsorship, or affiliation beyond the actual scope of your partnership.

### Product Screenshots and Data
- If including Snowflake product screenshots or UI images, obtain permission and use current, approved screenshots.
- Do not fabricate or mock up Snowflake product interfaces.

---

## 5. Approval Process

### Getting Official Assets
- Request official logo files and brand guidelines from your Snowflake partner contact or through the Snowflake partner portal.
- Use only the assets provided; do not source the logo from web searches or third-party sites.

### Review and Sign-Off
- Before distributing or presenting any co-branded materials, submit the deck to Snowflake's partner marketing team for review and approval.
- Allow adequate lead time (typically at least 1-2 weeks) for the review cycle.
- Incorporate any feedback and obtain written approval before finalizing.
