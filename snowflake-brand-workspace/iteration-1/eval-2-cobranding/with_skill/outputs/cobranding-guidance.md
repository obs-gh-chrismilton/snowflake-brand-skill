# Co-Branding Slide Deck Guidance: Your Company + Snowflake

This document provides comprehensive guidance for creating a co-branded slide deck that features both your company logo and Snowflake's logo, based on the official Snowflake brand guidelines.

---

## 1. Logo Placement Rules

### General Principles
- All logos must be **center-aligned** with each other.
- Logos must be **spaced equally** from one another and from surrounding content.
- Logos should appear **optically the same size** — match visual weight, not just pixel dimensions. A heavier or denser logo may need to be scaled down slightly so it does not visually dominate the Snowflake logo.

### Supported Layouts
Snowflake supports four co-branding arrangements. For a two-logo slide deck, use one of the following:

| Layout | Description | Best For |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Horizontal co-partnership** | Logos side by side on the same line | Title slides, headers, footers |
| **Vertical co-partnership** | Logos stacked vertically | Narrow spaces, sidebar placements |

For decks that include additional partner logos beyond the two, use the multi-partnership variants:

| Layout | Description | Best For |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Horizontal multi-partnership** | Multiple logos in a horizontal row | Title slides with 3+ partners |
| **Vertical multi-partnership** | Multiple logos stacked vertically | Partner acknowledgment slides |

### Clear Space
Maintain minimum clear space on all sides of the Snowflake logo equal to the **x-height of the Snowflake logotype**. Your company logo should have equivalent breathing room. No text, graphics, or other logos should encroach on this clear space.

## 2. Which Snowflake Logo Variant to Use

Snowflake provides two official logo variants. Choose based on the slide background:

| Background | Snowflake Logo Variant |
|------------|----------------------|
| White or light patterned/dotted backgrounds | **Snowflake Blue logo** (color version) |
| Snowflake Blue backgrounds, dark backgrounds, color-overlay images, non-white specialty items | **White logo** (reverse version) |

### Available Logo Files
The official Snowflake logo files are bundled in the brand assets:

- **SVG (recommended for presentations):** `assets/logos/Snowflake Logo/Digital/SVG/`
  - `snowflake-logo-color-rgb.svg` — full color logo with wordmark
  - `snowflake-logo-reverse-rgb.svg` — white logo with wordmark
  - `snowflake-bug-color-rgb.svg` — icon only, color
  - `snowflake-bug-reverse-rgb.svg` — icon only, white
- **PNG (if SVG is not supported):** `assets/logos/Snowflake Logo/Digital/PNG/` — available at @1x and @2x resolutions
- **EPS (for print production):** `assets/logos/Snowflake Logo/Print/EPS/` — CMYK versions

### Logo Don'ts
Never do any of the following to the Snowflake logo:
- Distort, stretch, or skew it
- Rotate it
- Recolor it or apply color effects
- Add shadows, glows, or outlines
- Place it on busy or low-contrast backgrounds
- Use outdated logo versions
- Remove the registration mark (R) from the bug/icon

### Registration Mark
The Snowflake bug (snowflake icon) must always include the (R) registration mark. When the bug is displayed at a large size, the registration mark should be one-third the size of an arrow leg, positioned at the center point of the 2 and 3 o'clock arrows' intersection.

## 4. Typography

### Headlines and Display Text
- **Font:** TEXTA typeface family
- Use for slide titles, section headers, and navigation labels
- Heavy weight for buttons and key CTAs

### Body and Subhead Text
- **Font:** Lato (freely available via Google Fonts)
- Use for body copy, bullet points, descriptions, and captions
- Clean and highly legible at all sizes

### Fallback Fonts
If TEXTA is not available in your presentation software, substitute with a geometric sans-serif such as **Montserrat** or **Nunito Sans**. Note that this is not an official Snowflake substitution but is a reasonable approximation.

## 5. Slide Design Recommendations

### Title Slide
- Place the Snowflake logo and your company logo side by side (horizontal co-partnership layout), centered on the slide.
- Ensure both logos are optically the same size.
- Use Snowflake Blue (`#29B5E8`) as the primary accent color for any headers or dividers.
- Body text in Midnight (`#000000`) using Lato.

### Content Slides
- Use Snowflake Blue for header bars, section dividers, and highlights.
- Body text in Midnight using Lato font.
- Secondary colors (Valencia Orange, Purple Moon, Star Blue) can be used for charts, icons, and accent elements.
- For dark-background slides, use tertiary colors (Iceberg, Winter, Violet Dawn) and switch to the white Snowflake logo variant.

### Data Visualization Slides
When including charts or graphs, use this recommended color sequence for data series:

1. Snowflake Blue (`#29B5E8`) — primary series
2. Valencia Orange (`#FF9F36`) — secondary series
3. Purple Moon (`#7D44CF`) — tertiary series
4. Star Blue (`#71D3DC`) — fourth series
5. First Light (`#D45B90`) — fifth series
6. Windy City (`#8A999E`) — sixth series / neutral

This ordering maximizes contrast between adjacent series while remaining on-brand.

### Closing/Contact Slide
- Return to the co-branded logo arrangement used on the title slide for visual bookending.
- CTAs and emphasis text should use Snowflake Blue.

## 8. Brand Compliance Checklist

Before finalizing the co-branded slide deck, verify every item below:

- [ ] Primary brand color is exactly Snowflake Blue (`#29B5E8`), not an approximation
- [ ] Snowflake logo has proper clear space on all sides (equal to the x-height of the logotype)
- [ ] Correct Snowflake logo variant is used for each slide background (color on light, white on dark)
- [ ] Partner logos are center-aligned and optically the same size as the Snowflake logo
- [ ] Typography uses Lato for body text (and TEXTA for headlines if available)
- [ ] Button text (if any) is ALL CAPS with a maximum of 13 characters
- [ ] Buttons use pill-shape styling (80px radius for desktop, 64px for social)
- [ ] Dark background slides use tertiary palette colors (Iceberg, Winter, Violet Dawn)
- [ ] No unapproved color modifications have been made to the Snowflake logo
- [ ] The Snowflake bug includes the registration mark (R) wherever it appears
- [ ] Data visualizations follow the recommended color sequence
- [ ] The 2025 PowerPoint template was used as the starting foundation
